# BasicRPG — Minimal Third-Person RPG Starter (Unity 6, URP)

A clean, minimal RPG-mechanics foundation to build on: third-person camera, walk,
sprint, jump, health, stamina, an on-screen HUD, a proximity interaction system
(talk to NPCs, open chests / metal caches / doors, pick up items), an inventory
(store, use consumables, equip weapon/armor, collect metals), basic melee combat
(attack, block, dodge) against patrolling enemies that drop loot, an allomancy
framework (burn metals, drain reserves, drink to refill), and a save/load system
(F5/F9) that persists the player's state. The working basics.

> The Ashwalker/Mistborn project is preserved untouched at
> `/home/ladams/Projects/Ashwalker-master/` for later.

## Open the project

1. Open **Unity Hub**.
2. **Add → Add project from disk** → select `/home/ladams/Projects/BasicRPG/`.
3. Unity opens it with editor **6000.5.1f1** and imports URP + the scripts.
   Wait for the import/compile to finish (bottom-right spinner).

> The project has no `ProjectSettings/` beyond the version file — Unity generates
> the rest on first open.

## Build the scene (one click)

Unity can't be driven from the command line in this environment, so the scene is
generated by an editor menu item:

1. Top menu: **RPG → Build Starter Scene**.
2. Watch the **Console** for `[RPGBuilder] ...` lines. It creates:
   - `Assets/Settings/URP.asset` + `Assets/Settings/URP_Renderer.asset` (URP assigned
     globally — so nothing renders pink),
   - `Assets/Scenes/Starter.unity` (ground, obstacles, the player — a CharacterController capsule
     with an Erbium humanoid model + locomotion Animator child — camera with URP
     post-processing on, directional light, HUD with health + stamina bars, an NPC, a chest,
     a metal cache, a door, scattered item pickups, patrolling enemies with world health bars,
     the metal wheel + Pewter + Tin allomancy effects, and the interaction UI — prompt,
     dialogue panel, loot toast, inventory panel),
   - `Assets/Settings/Items/*.asset` — `ItemSO` definitions (metals, consumables, equipment),
   - and adds `Starter.unity` to **Build Settings at index 0**.

If you see a red `[RPGBuilder] Cannot build while in Play mode` line, exit Play mode
and run the menu again.

### Per-metal test scenes

Each metal with a unique self-effect has its own dedicated test scene, built by a
separate menu item (each shares the same player/camera/HUD/allomancy scaffolding as
Starter, then adds tuned content). **Every test scene runs a guided, step-by-step tutorial
on load** that **freezes the game** while it teaches (so you can't die over and over while
reading): it walks a list of steps, showing one instruction at a time, and only advances
once you actually do the thing — press Tab → open the wheel → select the metal → press B to
burn → perform the metal's action (push an anchor, feel overload, suppress a Thug…). Menu
steps hold the world paused (`timeScale 0`); action steps release it so you can move. Press
**Backspace** to skip the whole tutorial.

- **RPG → Build Tin Test Scene** → `Assets/Scenes/TinTest.unity` — a pitch-dark arena with
  alcoves (enemies behind walls), bright enemy materials, patrolling enemies, and a glowing
  bright-light hazard. Tests Tin night vision, scent pings, vibration, and sensory overload.
- **RPG → Build Pewter Test Scene** → `Assets/Scenes/PewterTest.unity` — a stationary high-HP
  damage dummy, a platform gap clearable only with Pewter's jump, and attacking enemies.
  Tests Pewter's ~2.5× melee strength, held-wounds survivability, the gap-jump, and the drag crash.
- **RPG → Build Iron/Steel Test Scene** → `Assets/Scenes/IronSteelTest.unity` — scattered metal
  anchor cubes, a wall to push off, a raised and a far anchor to pull toward, and enemies to
  shove. Tests Steelpush (F) and Ironpull (Q).
- **RPG → Build Zinc/Brass Test Scene** → `Assets/Scenes/ZincBrassTest.unity` — enemies ringed
  just outside normal detect range. Tests Riot (Zinc swarm) and Soothe (Brass calm).
- **RPG → Build Copper/Bronze Test Scene** → `Assets/Scenes/CopperBronzeTest.unity` — Pewter-thug
  allomancer enemies. Tests Bronze pulse-sense and Copper suppression.

All test scenes start the basic metals at full reserve (no drinking needed).

The HUD (top-left) shows health (red) and stamina (green). Sprinting drains stamina;
release Shift and it regenerates after ~1s. When stamina hits zero you're "exhausted"
and can't sprint again until the bar recovers above ~15%.

## Interaction

Walk up to an NPC, chest, or metal cache. When you're in range a **prompt** appears at
the bottom-center ("Press E — Talk to Sazed", "Press E — Open Chest", …). Press **E**:

- **NPC** → a dialogue panel opens with the speaker's name + lines. Movement is locked
  while talking. Press **E**, **Space**, or **click** to advance; the panel closes after
  the last line and movement returns. (The same key that closes it won't instantly reopen it.)
- **Chest / Metal Cache** → the lid hinges open and a **toast** at the top-center shows what
  you found (e.g. "Found 3 iron ingots"). The prompt then reads "Close …" — press **E**
  again to shut it. Loot shows only once per chest.
- **Item pickup** (small colored cube) → "Press E — Pick up Iron" / "… Health Potion" /
  "… Iron Sword". Press **E** to add it to your inventory; the cube vanishes.
- **Door** → "Press E — Open Door" swings the leaf open so you can walk through; **E** again
  closes it.

## Inventory

Press **I** to toggle the inventory panel. While it's open, movement and camera orbit pause
and the mouse cursor appears so you can click slots.

- **Bag grid** — the items you're carrying, one per cell, with a count. Cells are tinted by
  category: grey = metal, green = consumable, blue = equipment.
- **Click a consumable** (e.g. Health Potion) to **use** it — restores health/stamina and the
  toast confirms. **Click equipment** (Iron Sword / Leather Armor) to **equip** it into the
  Weapon or Armor slot; clicking an equipped slot **unequips** it back to the bag.
- **Metals** (Iron, Steel, Pewter) are collectible resources — they stack in the bag and do
  nothing yet (they'll fuel allomancy/abilities later).

Press **I** again to close the panel and resume play.

## Save / Load

**F5** saves the player's state to `save.json` in Unity's `persistentDataPath`. **F9** loads it.
A toast confirms each ("Game saved" / "Game loaded"). Saved state covers:

- **Position + facing** (teleported back on load),
- **Health** and **Stamina**,
- **Inventory** — bag contents and equipped weapon/armor (items are stored by their stable
  `id` and resolved back to their `ItemSO` on load),
- **Allomancy** — every metal's reserve, the active metal, and whether you were burning.

This saves the *player's* state, not the *world*: re-running a builder re-creates consumed
pickups, and a saved game is meant to be loaded back in the same scene it was made in. Enemy
positions, dialogue progress, and chest state aren't persisted.

There are a few **enemies** (red capsules) in the field. Each patrols between a set of
waypoints until you get close, then **chases** you; when it's in melee range it winds up
(a brief yellow tint) and **attacks**, draining your health. A world-space health bar
above each enemy billboards toward the camera so you can read its HP at a glance.

- **Left Mouse** — melee swing. There's a short windup, then everything in a front arc
  within range takes damage. Damage = a base amount **plus your equipped weapon's power**
  (equip the Iron Sword from your inventory — I → click it — to hit harder).
- **Hold Right Mouse** — block. You move slowly while blocking and incoming hits deal
  only ~40% damage.
- **Tap C** — dodge dash. You dash in your current move direction (or forward) with brief
  invulnerability frames, so an enemy swing that lands during the dash does nothing. It
  costs stamina and has a short cooldown.
- Kill an enemy → "Enemy defeated" toast, and it **drops a metal pickup** (Iron/Steel/Pewter)
  you can collect for your inventory. Walk far enough away and a chasing enemy gives up and
  resumes patrolling.

Combat pauses while a dialogue or the inventory is open (so LMB there advances dialogue /
clicks inventory slots instead of swinging).

If your health hits **0**, the screen fades to a "You died — respawning…" overlay, input locks,
and after a few seconds you revive at your starting position with full health and stamina. (No
progress is lost except ground gained — there's no save system yet.)

## Allomancy

A port of Ashwalker's metal-burning system. The compact bottom-left **allomancy HUD** shows
the active metal's name + reserve bar + percent. Metals are picked from the **Metal Wheel**.

### Metal Wheel (Tab)

Press **Tab** to open the radial **metal wheel**: the screen mists over, **time freezes**
(enemies stop), the cursor unlocks, and a 16-slot wheel appears with the cursor at center.
Move the mouse — the slot in that direction scales up and its name shows in the center.
**Click** to confirm (that metal becomes active), or **Esc** / Tab again to cancel without
changing. The wheel closes, time resumes, and the HUD updates. Only one metal is active at a
time. The eight higher metals are greyed/locked (not selectable yet). The number keys **1–8**
remain a fast-path to select a basic metal without opening the wheel.

While the wheel is open, movement/combat/allomancy input is paused (the same lock used by
dialogue/inventory), so it won't open over those.

### Burning & fuel

- **B** — toggle **burning** the active metal. While burning, its reserve drains over time
  (each metal has a lore-based drain rate — Pewter is fast, Tin is slow). Burning auto-stops
  when the reserve runs dry.
- **X** — **drink** a metal: consumes one ingot of the active metal from your **inventory**
  and refills that metal's reserve (the Iron/Steel/Pewter you pick up are your fuel). A toast
  confirms; if you're out, it tells you.
- **RightAlt** — debug: refill every reserve to full.

### Flare (R — burn harder)

Hold **R** while burning any metal to **flare** it — burn the metal harder for a stronger
effect at the cost of draining its reserve much faster. The flare multiplier ramps up to ~1.8
and back down smoothly, so tapping R is a quick surge and holding it sustains the boost until
the reserve runs dry. What flaring does per metal:

- **Pewter** — even stronger hits, faster move/jump, faster mend (and faster drain + drag-crash
  countdown).
- **Tin** — brighter night vision, louder hearing, wider scent/vibration reach — but sensory
  overload builds faster too (the cost of sharper senses).
- **Iron/Steel** — harder push/pull launches and a stronger enemy shove.
- **Zinc/Brass** — a wider emotion aura (reaches more enemies).
- **Bronze** — hear allomantic pulses from farther away.
- **Copper** — a wider coppercloud (suppresses enemy allomancers in a bigger radius).

The HUD label reads "(flaring)" and the reserve bar brightens toward white while you hold R, so
the state is readable at a glance.

### Pewter (Thug) — physical enhancement

Select Pewter (Tab → click Pewter, or **3**) and press **B**:

- **Strength** — melee hits deal ~2.5× damage.
- **Speed / Jump** — move faster and jump higher (clear gaps you can't normally).
- **Toughness** — incoming hits deal ~20% less; a faint red vignette edges the screen.
- **Mend** — slowly heals you while burning (knits minor wounds faster).
- **Held wounds (lore)** — while Pewter burns, **half of each wound is held at bay**, not
  applied. So your health barely moves during a fight — the wounds were always real, Pewter
  just hid them. The instant Pewter stops (toggle B off, let the reserve run dry, or drag-crash),
  all that held shock lands as **one hit**. Dropping Pewter after a bad fight can drop you —
  that's the lore. Keep Pewter up while you're hurt.
- **Endurance** — sprinting drains **no stamina** while Pewter burns (the fatigue is *banked*).
  When you stop burning, that banked fatigue hits all at once *with interest* — burn briefly,
  pay a little; burn long, pay a lot.
- **Drag Crash** — burn Pewter for ~30+ seconds continuously, then stop (or let it run dry):
  a **drag crash** — the held wounds hit, you take crash damage, your stamina drops to zero /
  exhausted, and you're slowed for a few seconds ("Pewter drag crash!" toast). Short burns
  don't crash.
- **Flare** — hold **R** while burning to burn harder (bigger strength/speed/jump, faster
  mend) at the cost of draining the reserve faster.

Test it in the **Pewter Test Scene** (dummy, gap, attackers): hit the dummy, jump the gap,
take hits while burning (watch your health hold), then stop Pewter and feel the held wounds
land.

### Tin (Tineye) — enhanced senses

Select Tin (Tab → click Tin, or **4**) and press **B**:

- **Night vision** — the world brightens (URP exposure boost) so you can see in near-dark;
  contrast/saturation lift slightly, the far clip extends, and the FOV narrows a touch (focus).
- **Hearing** — `AudioListener` volume rises.
- **Scent** — directional pings at enemy positions (audible through walls).
- **Vibration** — moving enemies within range produce a faint camera shake.
- **Sensory Overload (the cost)** — a bright light (or loud noise) nearby while burning Tin
  fills an overload meter: the screen whites out, bloom flares, audio muffles, and movement
  slows. Step away and it recovers (lingering pain fades even after Tin stops).
- **World goes dull** — when the Tin reserve runs dry, the world briefly desaturates as the
  senses snap off.

Tin has only Off/Burning states for now (heartbeat deferred; flaring works — see Flare above).
Test it in the **Tin Test Scene** (pitch-dark arena + glowing hazard).

### Iron & Steel (Coinshot / Skimmer) — push & pull on metal

The signature Mistborn mobility. **It only works on metal**: only objects carrying a
`MetalAnchor` (the metal cubes — and **enemies**, which are metal-armored) can be pushed/pulled;
non-metal scenery (walls, platforms, the ground) is ignored by both the camera raycast and the
in-range scan. Metal objects **look metal** — anchor cubes and armored enemies use a full-metallic,
high-smoothness URP/Lit material so they read as reflective metal vs matte scenery.

**Metallurgic sight (see all metal):** while you burn Iron or Steel, a blue line is drawn from
your chest to **every** metal anchor in range — the Mistborn "blue lines to many metals." The
**primary** (the one you'll actually push/pull) is bright and thick (cool blue for Steel/push,
warm gold for Iron/pull); **secondaries** are dim and thin, with **brightness encoding camera
alignment** (how much you're looking at them) and **thickness encoding force** (how close/strong
they are). So at a glance you read both "the camera likes this" and "physics likes this."

**Targeting:** the primary is the anchor the camera points at (a raycast through the screen
centre, ignoring your own body) — precise and, by nature, line-of-sight. If nothing is clearly
aimed at, the primary is the best-scored anchor in range using a power-law
`score = forceProxy × alignment^assistStrength` (camera intent weighted by a power so off-centre
anchors fall off sharply; forceProxy = distance attenuation so nearer anchors win ties). In-range
**secondary anchors then bend the push/pull *direction* slightly** (metal-field awareness),
weighted by their relative force and capped — they shape the vector but never add force
magnitude, preserving the intent-vs-field distinction.

**Line of sight (the conscious decision):** sight *perceives* all metal through walls, but
*applying* the force requires line-of-sight — a chest→target ray must be unobstructed (your own
body is skipped). If the primary is behind a wall, its line tints **red** and no force is applied:
you can **see** metal through a wall, but you can't push/pull through it. (`requireLineOfSight`
can be disabled to allow through-wall force for experimentation.)

**Force:** the model is ported from Ashwalker's carefully-tuned push/pull math. Origin is the
chest, target is the anchor's centre; force uses a **plateau + smoothstep-tail** distance
attenuation (full force inside `plateauRange`, smooth falloff to zero at `effectiveRange`, hard
cutoff beyond — no discontinuous jump, no mushy near-field) with heavier anchors reaching a bit
farther, and a sub-precision guard treats below-epsilon force as exactly zero (silent jitter, not
corruption, is the real danger). Static metal (walls/cubes) is *anchored* — it doesn't move, you
launch off it; armored **enemies** are *loose* — shoving them also recoils you (mass-split).
Direction is recomputed every frame, so diagonal/arced pushes produce smooth parabolic paths
under gravity. The launch bleeds off with drag (an impulse, not a permanent speed).

- **Steel burning + hold F (Steelpush)** — launch yourself *away* from the targeted anchor
  (push off a wall behind you to fly forward, or off the ground to launch up). If the anchor is
  an enemy, it's shoved the opposite way.
- **Iron burning + hold Q (Ironpull)** — yank yourself *toward* the targeted anchor (pull up to
  a high anchor, or across a gap to a far one).
- **Hold R to flare** — harder push/pull and a stronger enemy shove, at faster drain.

Pushing/pulling drains the burning metal faster than passive burn. The active metal gates the
ability: F works only while Steel is burning, Q only while Iron is burning.

Test it in the **Iron/Steel Test Scene** (wall + anchors + raised/far anchors + enemies).

### Zinc & Brass (Riot / Soothe) — emotion aura

An aura around the player that retunes nearby enemies' aggression. No key to hold — just burn
the metal (**B**); the aura is active while it burns and enemies outside it (or when you stop)
snap back to normal.

- **Zinc burning (Riot)** — nearby enemies turn **hyper-aggressive**: wider detect range, faster
  attacks, quicker movement. They swarm you from farther off.
- **Brass burning (Soothe)** — nearby enemies turn **calm**: tiny detect range (they barely
  notice you), slow attacks, sluggish movement. You can stroll right past them.

Test it in the **Zinc/Brass Test Scene** (enemies ringed just outside normal detect range —
Brass lets you walk through, Zinc makes them swarm).

### Copper & Bronze (Smoker / Seeker) — hide & sense allomancy

Some enemies are **allomancers** (dark-red Pewter-thugs): while burning they buff themselves
(faster, hit harder). Copper and Bronze are the player's answers to that. Both are passive —
just burn the metal (**B**).

- **Bronze burning (Seeker)** — hear allomantic pulses. Nearby burning enemy allomancers emit a
  directional ping, and a toast reports how many are in range ("Bronze pulse: 2 allomancers
  nearby"). Suppressed enemies are silent.
- **Copper burning (Smoker)** — emit a **coppercloud** around you. Enemy allomancers inside it
  are **suppressed**: they stop burning and lose their buff (slow down, hit normally). A
  suppressed Thug visibly turns grey-blue and a toast flags it ("Coppercloud suppresses a
  Thug"); step out and it re-buffs (and turns red again). Hold **R** to flare the cloud wider.

Test it in the **Copper/Bronze Test Scene** (two Pewter-thugs + a normal grunt).

All eight basic metals now have effects (Iron, Steel, Pewter, Tin, Copper, Bronze, Zinc, Brass).
The eight higher metals (Gold, Electrum, Cadmium, Bendalloy, Aluminum, Duralumin, Chromium,
Nicrosil) still burn/drain with no effect yet — those come later.

## If something looks wrong

- **Anything pink/magenta** → re-run **RPG → Build Starter Scene** (re-assigns the URP
  asset). If still pink, check *Project Settings → Graphics* points at
  `Assets/Settings/URP.asset`.
